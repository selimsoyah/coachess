# CoaChess User Guide

Welcome to CoaChess! This guide will help you get started with the platform.

## üéØ For Coaches

### Getting Started

#### 1. Create Your Account
1. Go to the signup page
2. Select **Coach** as your role
3. Enter your email and password
4. Verify your email (check spam folder)

#### 2. Create Your First Lesson

1. From your dashboard, click **"Create Content"**
2. Choose **Lesson** for a full game with moves
3. Enter a descriptive title (e.g., "Scholar's Mate Example")
4. Paste your PGN notation
5. Watch the live preview to verify
6. Click **"Save Content"**

**Example PGN:**
```
[Event "Scholar's Mate"]
[Site "CoaChess"]
[Date "2025.10.25"]

1. e4 e5 2. Bc4 Nc6 3. Qh5 Nf6 4. Qxf7#
```

#### 3. Create Your First Puzzle

1. From your dashboard, click **"Create Content"**
2. Choose **Puzzle** for a position to solve
3. Enter a title (e.g., "Mate in 2")
4. Paste your FEN notation
5. Preview the position
6. Click **"Save Content"**

**Example FEN:**
```
r1bqkb1r/pppp1ppp/2n2n2/4p2Q/2B1P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNB1K1NR w KQkq - 4 4
```

#### 4. Invite a Player

1. Go to **Dashboard** ‚Üí **"Connections"**
2. Click **"Invite Player"**
3. Enter the player's email
4. Click **"Create Invite"**
5. Copy the invite link
6. Share it with your student via:
   - Email
   - Text message
   - Chat app
   - Any communication method

#### 5. Manage Your Content

**View All Content:**
- Go to **Dashboard** ‚Üí **"View Library"**
- See all your lessons and puzzles
- Use search to find specific content
- Filter by type (lessons/puzzles)

**Edit Content:**
1. In your library, click the edit icon (pencil)
2. Make your changes
3. Click **"Save Content"**

**Delete Content:**
1. In your library, click the delete icon (trash)
2. Confirm deletion

#### 6. Manage Connections

**View Connections:**
- Go to **Dashboard** ‚Üí **"Connections"**
- See all pending, active, and revoked connections

**Revoke a Connection:**
1. Find the player in your connections list
2. Click the revoke icon (circle with slash)
3. Confirm revocation

**Delete a Connection:**
1. Find the player in your connections list
2. Click the delete icon (trash)
3. Confirm deletion

## üí° Pro Tips

### For Coaches

**Creating Quality Content:**
- Use descriptive titles that explain the concept
- Add notes in your PGN comments (e.g., `{This move controls the center}`)
- Group similar content together (e.g., "Opening Series")
- Start with simpler puzzles and progress to harder ones

**Managing Students:**
- Keep track of which content you've assigned (use notes)
- Create a progression path for your students
- Mix lessons with puzzles for variety
- Regular check-ins help student progress

**Content Organization:**
- Use consistent naming (e.g., "Lesson 1: ", "Puzzle 1: ")
- Tag content by difficulty in the title
- Group content by theme (openings, tactics, endgames)

### For Players

**Studying Effectively:**
- Take your time with each lesson
- Try to predict the next move before revealing it
- Review lessons multiple times
- Ask your coach questions about confusing positions

**Solving Puzzles:**
- Don't rush to the answer
- Calculate variations mentally first
- Look for forcing moves (checks, captures, threats)
- Learn from mistakes - understand why a move fails

## üîç Understanding Chess Notation

### PGN (Portable Game Notation)

PGN records entire games:

```
1. e4 e5    (Move 1: White plays e4, Black plays e5)
2. Nf3 Nc6  (Move 2: White plays knight to f3, Black plays knight to c6)
3. Bc4 Bc5  (Move 3: White plays bishop to c4, Black plays bishop to c5)
```

**Common Symbols:**
- `K` = King
- `Q` = Queen
- `R` = Rook
- `B` = Bishop
- `N` = Knight
- (Pawns have no letter)
- `x` = Capture
- `+` = Check
- `#` = Checkmate
- `O-O` = Kingside castle
- `O-O-O` = Queenside castle

### FEN (Forsyth-Edwards Notation)

FEN describes a single position:

```
rnbqkbnr/pppppppp/8/8/8/8/PPPPPPPP/RNBQKBNR w KQkq - 0 1
```

- Lowercase = Black pieces
- Uppercase = White pieces
- Numbers = Empty squares
- `/` = Next rank (row)

You don't need to understand FEN in detail - just copy/paste it!

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### For Coaches

**Q: How many players can I connect with?**
A: Currently unlimited! The free tier supports many connections.

**Q: Can I delete content that I've assigned?**
A: Yes, but be aware that assigned content will no longer be accessible to players.

**Q: Can players see all my content?**
A: No, players only see content that you specifically assign to them.

**Q: How do I resend an invite?**
A: Go to Connections, find the pending invite, and click the copy icon to get the link again.

**Q: Can I edit content after assigning it?**
A: Yes! Changes will be reflected for all assignments.

### For Players

**Q: Can I connect with multiple coaches?**
A: Yes! Accept invites from any coaches you want to work with.

**Q: What happens if I decline an invite?**
A: The connection won't be created. You can't accept it later without a new invite.

**Q: Can I see my coach's entire content library?**
A: No, you only see content your coach assigns to you.

**Q: Can I message my coach?**
A: Messaging feature is coming soon!

## üÜò Troubleshooting

### "Not authenticated" error
- Log out and log back in
- Clear your browser cache
- Try a different browser

### Can't see the chess board
- Refresh the page
- Check your internet connection
- Try disabling browser extensions

### Invite link not working
- Make sure you're using the full link
- Log in before clicking the link
- Ask your coach to generate a new invite

### Content not saving
- Check that all required fields are filled
- Verify your chess notation is valid
- Check the validation message
- Try refreshing and saving again
